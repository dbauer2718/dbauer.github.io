About Me

I am a biophysicist with academic and industry research experience, where I have focused on building hardware and software tools to help answer scientific questions. I did my PhD at the University of California, San Francisco in Wallace Marshall's lab, where I studied noise in the flagellar length control circuit. When I'm not doing science, I enjoy running, playing piano, and reading science fiction.
