Cell Size Control in Stentor coeruleus

Cells are incredibly diverse in size and shape, ranging from 1-2μm in the case of E. coli, and up to several hundred mm in the case of axons. This diversity in size and shape has a sizable impact on biological function. A breakdown of cell size control is often associated with cancer, leading some to diagnose cancer based on abnormal cell shape alone. Stentor coeruleus is a giant single cell organism (up to 1mm in length!) that has the remarkable ability to regenerate. Because of its size and regenrative ability, we think this organism has a lot of potential to contribute to the open question of how cells regulate their size. We designed and built microfluidic chambers capable of keeping Stentor in a single focal plane for long term imaging, so that we can observe the regeneration process.
